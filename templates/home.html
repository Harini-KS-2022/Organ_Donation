Organ Donation and Procurement Management System

Living Donation

Living donation takes place when a living person donates an organ (or part of an organ) for transplantation to another person.

Deceased Donation

Deceased organ donation is the process of giving an organ or a part of an organ, at the time of the donor’s death, for the purpose of transplantation to another person.

Tissue Donation

Donating tissue such as skin, bone, and heart valves can help heal and improve the quality of life for recipients. Cornea donation helps provide the gift of sight to up to two recipients.

Pediatric donation

Children often respond better to child-sized organs, which is why this type of donation differs slightly from other organ donations.

One person can save eight lives and enhance 75 others through organ, eye and tissue donation.

On an average 22 People die every day waiting for a life-saving organ.
